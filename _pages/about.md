This blog documents my learning path and hands-on practice across cybersecurity and local AI. I experiment with detection engineering, pentesting, threat hunting, digital forensics, and AI-powered automation that supports all of the above.

Everything published here is based on my own hands-on experience: configuring tools like Wazuh, pfSense, and Velociraptor; simulating real-world attacks in Active Directory environments; applying MITRE ATT&CK in lab-scale scenarios; and building AI agents and n8n workflows that make security work faster and fully private.

This blog also serves as a living reference for the configurations, decisions, and issues I've worked through.


## Who I Am

I'm a cybersecurity enthusiast and graduate student pursuing an M.S. in Digital Forensics and Cyber Investigation. I document hands-on experience in threat hunting, pentesting, digital forensics, and security operations using a custom-built home lab.

Lately I've been exploring how AI fits into a cybersecurity workflow, specifically running large language models locally with Ollama and building agentic automation with n8n. The goal is to use AI as a force multiplier while keeping sensitive data completely off cloud infrastructure.

My technical interests include:

- SIEM and log analysis (Wazuh, Sysmon)
- Incident response and endpoint forensics (Velociraptor, DFIR workflows)
- Red/purple team methodologies (Kali Purple, BloodHound, simulated adversaries)
- Secure lab infrastructure and automation (Proxmox, pfSense, Python)
- Local AI for security workflows (Ollama, Open WebUI, self-hosted LLMs)
- AI-powered automation and agents (n8n, agentic pipelines for threat intel and reporting)

The lab closely mimics enterprise conditions, including multiple VLANs, domain controllers, and monitored endpoints. The AI workstation side runs Kubuntu with a fully local, privacy-first stack.
